WHAT IS GEOBIOLOGY?

So far this semester we have been studying geology and Earth's lithosphere. But the lithosphere doesn't just exist by itself. It exists along with the other spheres of the Earth: the Atmosphere, the Hydrosphere, and the Biosphere. When we studied the Rock Cycle and the Water Cycle, we learned about some of the ways that the hydrosphere and atmosphere interact with the lithosphere. Now, we will learn about how the biosphere interacts with the lithosphere. Geobiology is about how life affects geology and how geology affects life.

Biology Review: Prokaryotes

Photo Credit: commons.wikimedia.org

Main Points:

Single-celled organism that lacks a defined nucleus, but has a "Nucleoid": an irregularly-shaped region where a single chromosomal, circular, double-stranded DNA molecule is located.

Prokaryotes are divided into two domains: Bacteria and Archea.

Bacteria: inhabit almost all environments where some liquid water is available and temperature is less than 140 degrees celsius; there is estimated to be five million trillion trillion bacteria in the world.

Archaea: very similar to bacteria in their cell structure and metabolism; have membrane lipids that are specific to archea, which helps them adapt to extreme environments.

Ribosomes are the only organelles in prokaryotes

Biology Review: Eukaryotes

Photo Credit: commons.wikimedia.org

Main Points:

Eukaryotic cells are cells that contain a nucleus and are usually larger than prokaryotic cells.

Organisms with eukaryotic cells are called eukaryotes, and they range from fungi to people.

Contain many more organelles than prokaryotic cells, such as: mitochondria, vacuoles and lysosomes.

What is needed for life to exist?

Life requires these three things:

-Source of energy. One source of energy on Earth is the sun.

-Organic Compounds. On earth, living things are made of compunds that are made of the following elements: hydrogen (H), carbon(C), sulfur(S), nitrogen(N), phosphorus(P), and oxygen(O).

-Liquid on the surface. Humans need liquid water to survive.
