How was the Universe Created?

13.5 Billion years ago: The Big Bang!

The universe began with a massive explosion that generated space and time, along with all of the matter and energy in the universe. It is unknown what caused this explosion, but it may have been inflation. At this point in time, there were no particles such as protons, neutrons, bosons, neutrinos, or electrons. This means that atoms did not exist because there was nothing to make them out of.

Less than a second after the Big Bang

As the universe continues to expand, it starts to cool. Basic forces, such as gravity, are now in existence. The universe now contains the fundamental particles of matter and energy: quarks, electrons, photons, neutrinos and others. At the end of this era protons and neutrons form, but atoms are still unable to form.

3 seconds after the Big Bang

The universe is now cool enough for the formation of three simple elements: hydrogen, helium and lithium. No other elements are able to form.
