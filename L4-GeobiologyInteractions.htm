HOW DOES GEOLOGY AND BIOLOGY INTERACT?

There are many processes on Earth that result in a cycle. For example, the processes of water on Earth result in the water cycle and the processes that create and change different types of rocks result in the rock cycle. In some other cycles, both geologic and biologic activity contribute to the processes in the cycle.

The Carbon Cycle

Photo Credit: ck12.org

The ocean is a major reservoir of carbon. Runoff, streams, and rivers gradually dissolve carbbon on rocks and bring that carbon to the ocean. The other reservoirs of carbon are the atmosphere, the biosphere and organic sediments like fossil fuels.

Carbon in the atmosphere

In the atmosphere, carbon exists mainly as the gas carbon dioxide. Carbon dioxide can end up in the atmosphere because it is a byproduct of: cellular respiration, decomposition of living organisms, volcanic eruptions, heating of limestone and heating of the ocean. Carbon also exists in the atmosphere as methane, which is released into the atmosphere when decomposition happens in the absence of oxygen, during some mining processes and by some types of agriculture.

Carbon leaves the atmosphere in several ways. Plants remove carbon dioxide during phototsynthesis. When ocean water cools, it dissolves carbon dioxide from the air.

Carbon in the ocean

When carbon dioxide dissolves in water, it forms carbonic acid. In areas where the ocean water is cooler, more carbon dissolved and there is more carbonic acid. Carbonic acid helps regulate the pH balance in the ocean. Carbon can be held in its ocean reservoir for thousands of years.

Carbon in the biosphere

Photosynthetic algae and bacteria can use bicarbonate ions near the surface of the ocean to synthesize organic compounds. Organic compounds help make up the cells of living organisms. Carbon cycles back into the ocean and atmosphere through cellular respiration.

The Nitrogen Cycle

Photo Credit: ck12.org

The atmosphere is the largest reservoir of nitrogen on Earth and it consists of 78% nitrogen. Nitrogen passes from the atmosphere into the soil and then thorugh several organisms before returning to the atmosphere. Plants and other producers use nitrogen to synthesize nitrogen-containing compounds, such as chlorophyll, proteins and nucleic acids. Other organisms use these compounds. The process of converting nitrogen gas into nitrate ions is called "nitrogen fixation".
